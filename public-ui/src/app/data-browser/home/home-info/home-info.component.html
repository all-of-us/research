What are Electronic Health Records (EHR)?

Electronic health records Health records are the information collected when you get health care. The data for/from all participants who consent to link their EHR data will be saved to AllOfUS Data Research Center (DRC). EHR data will be accessed longitudinally throughout the life of the program. The initial data collected include demographics, ICD-9, CPT codes, medications charts, labs etc.

What are Survey Modules?

Surveys are grouped into modules, based on the types of questions they include. Currently, participants are asked to complete three survey modules: The Basics, Overall Health, and Lifestyle. Additional surveys will be included as they are developed.

What is Participant Provided Information (PPI)?

When participants enroll in the program, they are asked to respond to a number of surveys. Participants are asked to answer honestly and to the best of their knowledge. Surveys are available in both English and Spanish.

What is the OMOP Data Model?

AllofUS program employs Observational Medical outcomes Partnership (OMOP) Common Data Model Version 5 infrastructure to ensure feasibility and standardization across EHR data
The Observational Medical Outcomes Partnership (OMOP) was a public-private partnership established to inform the appropriate use of observational healthcare databases for studying the effects of medical products. Over the course of the 5-year project and through its community of researchers from industry, government, and academia, OMOP successfully achieved its aims to: 1) conduct methodological research to empirically evaluate the performance of various analytical methods on their ability to identify true associations and avoid false findings, 2) develop tools and capabilities for transforming, characterizing, and analyzing disparate data sources across the health care delivery spectrum, and 3) establish a shared resource so that the broader research community can collaboratively advance the science.

Where can I learn more about the development of the Instrument?

Instruments were vetted by a consortium of experts and piloted with focus groups to ensure readability and accessibility.
